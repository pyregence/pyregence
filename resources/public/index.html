Project Biography

Current wildfire science and forecasting models lack capability to provide scientifically rigorous, timely and location specific insights for situational awareness of wildfire threats to and from the electric grid. The fire physics supporting the state-level models fail to address the substantial changes in fire behaviour driven by higher fuel loads, more frequent extreme weather events and development in the wildland urban interface throughout California. Furthermore, wildfire modelling is often in a black box and/or not scientifically reviewed resulting in a lack of transparency into the basis of many critical, often life-saving decisions.

Pyregence is a consortium funded by the California Energy Commission to focus on the challenge of wildfire impacts to and from the electric grid. Pyregence aims to advance fire behaviour science, enhance cutting-edge risk models and operationalize the analysis for electric utilities and adaptation planning for California’s Fifth Climate Change Assessment. The consortium will release all software code as open source. Throughout the development of new software, a diversity of specialists will compare and contrast approaches to addressing technical challenges and converge on the optimal path to the next generation of wildfire models.

Extreme Weather

Electric utilities need better weather forecasts to improve situational awareness of wildfire threats to and from the grid. Pyregence will support electric utilities by developing a methodology for optimal location of weather stations and piloting an upper-air profiler over a fire season to scout for early signs of impending extreme wind events.

Advance Wildfire Science

Current fire models fail to address the fundamental physics behind contemporary wildfire events in the State of California. This team aims to deliver fundamental scientific advances to give rise to the next generation of wildfire models, including a new fuel mapping technique and fire spread model based derived from fire physics.

Wildfire Forecasts

Current wildfire forecasting software fails to take into account fundamental fire physics and often uses a singular approach in modelling the complexities of wildfires in California. Project Pyregence will integrate multiple best practice wildfire models into a single open source software interface to provide IOUs with the best possible 0-7 day fire forecasts that are vital to managing a more reliable, safe, cost effective and resilient electric grid.

Wildfire Scenarios

California is faced with a challenging ‘new normal’: an increased risk of megafires resulting in catastrophic impacts to society over the coming century. The Pyregence consortium will develop wildfire scenarios under a changing climate to inform mitigation and adaptation planning for a more safe, cost- effective, reliable and resilient electric utility grid.

Pyregence Consortium

The consortium brings together wildfire scientists and technologists partners from leading research institutions from industry, academia, and government.

Everything open source because it’s good

The Pyregence Consortium is committed to transparency and reproducibility throughout our systems because we recognize these as necessary ingredients for successful science-based decision making. No one trusts a black box, and that is why all of our software from our weather pipeline and high performance fire models to our dynamic web interface is being developed and released under the strongest open source license currently available: the GPLv3 (link: https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html). This ensures that any contributions to Pyregence's tools will remain in the open source domain indefinitely and may be reused and improved upon by the next generation of modelers, programmers, and fire scientists.
